üåê Deploy to GitHub Pages

This is the easiest and FREE way to get your website online!

  • Go to GitHub.com and create an account (if you don't have one)
  • Click the "+" icon (top right) ‚Üí "New repository"
  • Name it: KristinaCV
  • Make it Public
  • Click "Create repository"
  • Click "uploading an existing file"
  • Drag and drop ALL your files
  • Click "Commit changes"
  • Go to Settings ‚Üí Pages
  • Under "Branch", select "main" ‚Üí Save
  • Wait 2-3 minutes, then your site will be at:
    https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/KristinaCV/
‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: After deploying, you'll need to generate new QR codes with your actual website URL! Continue to Step 3.
3

üì± Generate Your QR Codes

Now that you have your real website URL, create QR codes:

  • Open qr-generator.html in a text editor (Notepad, VS Code, etc.)
  • Find line ~135: text: "https://yourusername.github.io/KristinaCV/"
  • Replace with YOUR actual URL (from step 2)
  • Find line ~144: text: "https://yourusername.github.io/KristinaCV/chatbot.html"
  • Replace with YOUR actual chatbot URL
  • Save the file
  • Open qr-generator.html in your browser
  • Click both download buttons
  • You now have 2 QR code images!
4

üìÑ Add QR Codes to Documents

Method 1 (Easy - Screenshot):

  • Open cv-updated.html in your browser
  • Take a screenshot of the page
  • Open in image editor or Word
  • Paste your QR code images over the placeholders
  • Save as PDF

Method 2 (Code - Better quality):

  • Open cv-updated.html in text editor
  • Find: <div class="qr-placeholder-small">
  • Replace with: <img src="Website-QR-Code.png" style="width: 80px;">
  • Do the same for the chatbot QR
  • Open in browser and print to PDF
